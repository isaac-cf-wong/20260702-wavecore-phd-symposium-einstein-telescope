## Abstract

The direct detection of gravitational waves has opened a new observational
window onto the Universe, enabling the study of black holes, neutron stars, and
extreme astrophysical phenomena through space-time vibrations themselves.
Following the success of the current generation of detectors, the next major
step for the field is the Einstein Telescope (ET), a proposed European
third-generation gravitational-wave observatory with substantially improved
sensitivity and detection capability.

In this keynote, I will provide an overview of gravitational-wave science and
the scientific vision of the Einstein Telescope, highlighting how future
detectors will transform our understanding of the Universe. Beyond the
astrophysical discoveries, I will focus on the significant technological and
data-analysis challenges associated with next-generation detectors, including
ultra-precise sensing, environmental and correlated noise mitigation,
large-scale signal processing, real-time inference, and AI-assisted data
analysis.

The Einstein Telescope represents not only a breakthrough for astronomy and
fundamental physics, but also a frontier challenge in sensing, signal
processing, statistical inference, computing, and intelligent systems. I will
discuss current developments and open challenges in the field, as well as
emerging opportunities for interdisciplinary research in next-generation
gravitational-wave science.
